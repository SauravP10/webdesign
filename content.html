What is HCI?

Human computer interaction is the study design, evaluation, and implementation of interactive computing systems for human use and the major phenomena surrounding them. Consist of three parts: the person, the computer, the way they work together

What is an User Interface

User interface design refers to graphical user interfaces and other forms that designers build in software to create interfaces for computerized devices that users find easy to use and pleasurable.

What makes a Good User Interface?

User interface should be enjoyable, they should communicate brand values. Good design is emotional design that evokes emotion which results in experiences. Users judge designs quickly and care about usability and likeability. People care about getting their task done easily and with minimum effort.

What does your design need to take into account?

Consider the fact that users judge designs quickly and take into account usability and likeability
Process of interaction design:
1. Identifying needs and requirements
2. Developing designs that fulfill those requirements
3. Building interactive products of the design so that they can be used
4. Evaluate the ‘building’ process throughout

Usability Goals

Effectiveness:
The system needs to be effective at what it is meant to do

Efficiency:
Is the interactive product efficient in what it does?

Safety:
Is the interactive product safe to use? This involves two aspects; external and internal safety. External safety is concerned with the surrounding environment of the interactive product. Hazardous objects should not be near the product. The internal aspect of the safety goal is to make sure that the users are safe in a way that if they make a mistake they can easily ‘re-do’ this click and their work/progress is not lost or deleted.

Utility:
This goal aims to provide the user with the correct functionality a system can offer.

Learnability:
Is the system easy to learn?

Memorability:
Interface support is an essential part of any interactive product. The aim of it is to aid users remember how tasks/functions are carried out.
